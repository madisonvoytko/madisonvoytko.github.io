August 2025 - Present

Undergraduate Researcher

Neu Research Lab, CU Boulder

Building mechanical systems to study how joints break down under load—and how to stop it.

The challenge: create a device that can mechanically load tiny pieces of cartilage and bone the same way your knee does when you walk, but small enough to fit under a microscope. I helped design a pressure-driven system using layered silicone chambers that push and pull on tissue samples in real time.

After building and testing a dozen devices, we've proven the system works consistently—each one behaves almost identically, which is critical for reliable experiments.

August 2025 - Present

Test & Systems Engineer

3D-Printed Brain Phantom Project (CaliberMRI Partnership)

Making sure a 3D-printed brain phantom can survive the real world—and actually work in an MRI machine.

Our team partnered with CaliberMRI to develop a biomimetic brain phantom for MRI calibration. My job was to figure out where and how it would fail. I designed test protocols to push the device to its limits—checking if seals would leak, if thin walls would collapse, if the whole thing would hold up under handling and shipping.

I documented every potential failure mode, ranked them by risk, and presented findings to the CaliberMRI team so they could make informed design decisions. It's the unglamorous work that keeps medical devices from falling apart when they matter most.
